Cold Brew

If you crave a good iced coffee in the summer, but loathe the way so many end up tasting watery or overly bitter, then there's only one solution: cold brew coffee. This method guarantees a smooth and icy cup, every time. Making cold brew coffee is no great secret, nor does it require the ninja-level skills of a trained barista in order to master. You don't even need much special equipment beyond a large container for making the coffee and a strainer.

Ingredients

  • Coffee beans
  • coffee grinder
  • Water
  • Ice

Directions

  • Step 1:
    • Add whole beans to a coffee grinder and grind to a medium to coarse grind.
  • Step 2:
    • Measure out the coffee grinds and room-temperature water in a ratio of 1/4 cup coffee to 1 cup water.
  • Step 3:
    • Add the coffee grounds and water to a pitcher or large measuring cup. Stir to combine. Cover with a lid, and leave the coffee at room temperature, agitating periodically, for 12 hours or overnight. Strain the coffee through a coffee filter. Discard the grinds and the filter.
  • Step 4:
    • Serve the coffee with ice and cream depending on your taste !
